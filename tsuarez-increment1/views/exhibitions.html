Exhibition 1

This exhibition draws together over 200 artifacts recovered from sunken postal vessels dating between 1840 and 1940, tracing the intimate correspondences that never reached their destinations. Visitors move through a series of dimly lit alcoves, each representing a different sea route, where waterlogged letters, logbooks, and personal effects are suspended in conservation glass like frozen moments of longing. The collection invites a meditation on distance, loss, and the strange persistence of human voices across depths that were never meant to preserve them.

Exhibition 2

This exhibition catalogs sounds that were documented once and never heard again: the creak of a demolished bridge, the ambient hum of a factory floor on its last operating day, the particular silence of a village evacuated before a flood. Each sound is represented not through playback but through hand-drawn waveform illustrations, field notes, and small sculptural objects meant to approximate the sensation of hearing. It is less a celebration of sound than a museum of its absence, and visitors frequently report leaving in an unusual, unnameable quiet.
